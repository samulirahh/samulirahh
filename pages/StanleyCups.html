Stanley Cups

1983-84 Was the season Edmonton Oilers won their first Stanley Cup defeating the New York Islanders in the fifth game of the playoff finals. Captained by Wayne Gretzky alongside players like Jari Kurri and Mark Messier.

1984-85 Was the season Edmonton Oilers completed their first back-to-back Stanley Cup wins by beating the Philadelphia Flyers. Captained by Wayne Gretzky this team was voted by fans as the greatest NHL team of all time.

1986-87 Was the season Edmonton Oilers defeated the Philadelphia Flyers to win their 3rd Stanley Cup in 4 seasons. The Flyers managed to take the finals to game 7 but the Oilers managed to win.

1987-88 Was the season Edmonton Oilers won their second back-to-back Stanley Cups. Captained by Wayne Gretzky in the legendary players final season with the Oilers, The Oilers sweeped the Boston Bruins in a 4 game series. The goalkeeper Grant Fuhr became the first Oilers goalie to win the Vezina Trophy, which is awarded to the best goalie at the end of each season.

1989-90 Was the season Edmonton Oilers won their last Stanley Cup to date. Missing their previous Captain Wayne Gretzky, who got traded to the LA Kings the Oilers managed to beat the Boston Bruins in the playoff finals with star players like Jari Kurri, Mark Messier, Glenn Anderson and Grant Fuhr stepping up to take the Edmonton Oilers to Stanley Cup glory.
